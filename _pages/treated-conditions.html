Achilles Tendinitis

Achilles Tendinitis is the inflammation of your Achilles tendon. The Achilles Tendon begins where the two calf muscles meet. It run downs the leg and attaches at the heel of the foot. Overuse, improper foot support, and arthritis are among the causes of Achilles Tendinitis. Patients generally feel pain in their heel while performing certain activities. They may also feel tightness or tenderness throughout the heel.

The chiropractors at Noble Choice Chiropractic has treated Achilles Tendinitis with great success through the combined use of: ART, Graston, therapeutic tape, laser therapy, ultrasound therapy and chiropractic.

Bursitis

Bursitis is the inflammation of a bursa. Bursa is a fluid-like sac that is found between muscles or tendons and a bony prominence. The purpose of a bursa is to prevent friction between these structures. Bursae may be found in the shoulder, elbow, hip and knee, just to name a few. Bursitis can be caused by overuse, trauma, rheumatoid arthritis, gout, and infection. Some symptoms include: Pain, swelling, redness, stiffness, and tenderness to the touch in the affected area.

At Noble Choice Chiropractic, we combine some or all of the following treatments: Active Release Technique®, teaching you stretches and exercises, Graston Technique, ultrasound therapy or laser therapy, adjustments or manipulation to the affected joint, and therapeutic taping.

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome is caused when the median nerve in the forearm becomes compressed within the Carpal Tunnel. Repetitive motions such as typing on a keyboard, sewing, writing, playing some musical instruments, and using hand tools are causes of Carpal Tunnel Syndrome. Patients usually have numbness and tingling through the hand and fingers excluding the pinky and pinky side of the ring finger. Patients may also lose strength and coordination throughout the hand.

The chiropractors at Noble Choice Chiropractic have treated Carpal Tunnel Syndrome with great success through the use of: Active Release Technique®, Graston Technique®, therapeutic tape, and Chiropractic.

Back Pain

There are a myriad of reasons for back pain. Patients can have acute pain due to an increase in activities such as shoveling, raking or even an injury. Chronic pain may be due to multiple factors such as arthritis, disc herniations or working certain jobs with extended periods of sitting, driving or standing. Because there are many different conditions that manifest as generalized back pain, it is important to diagnose and treat the cause of the condition, not the symptoms of the condition.

The chiropractors at Noble Choice Chiropractic have treated back pain with success through the use of: Active Release Technique®, Graston Technique®, therapeutic taping, ultrasound therapy and chiropractic adjustments.

Disc Herniations

In the spine, cartilaginous discs are located between each vertebrae. These discs act as cushions, protecting vertebra from trauma or injury. A healthy disc also allows movement of the spine in a normal, pain-free, fashion. There are 23 spinal discs in the spine but the largest discs lie in the Lumbar spine. These lumbar discs are most commonly herniated. A disc herniation occurs when the disc protrudes from its proper space. This can cause inflammation and pressure on the nearby nerves. Improper ergonomics, trauma, or repetitive motions and overuse are common causes of disc herniations. Patients can feel anything from a mild tingle to a disabling sting or burn. Pain is usually limited to one side of the body and is most typically a shooting pain down the leg, in cases of lumbar disc herniations.

The chiropractors at Noble Choice Chiropractic have treated disc herniations with success through the use of: Active Release Technique®, Graston Technique®, therapeutic taping, and chiropractic adjustments.

Golfer's Elbow aka Medial Epicondylitis

Golfer's Elbow is a condition caused by repetitive and forceful overuse of the flexor and pronator muscles of the wrist and forearm. These muscles help to rotate the forearm and flex the wrist. Pain is felt from the inner (medial) elbow down to the wrist. The pain with golfer's elbow is exacerbated when flexing and/or internally rotating the wrist against resistance.

The chiropractors at Noble Choice Chiropractic have treated Golfer's Elbow with success through the use of: Active Release Technique®, Graston Technique®, therapeutic taping, and chiropractic adjustments.

Headache

There are many different types of headaches, ranging from severe migraines to cluster headaches. Each headache has specific pain patterns, severity and aggravating factors that help the chiropractors diagnose your condition. By far the most common type of headache is called a Tension Headache. These headaches are caused by tight muscles in the head, neck, and/or shoulders. The tightness in your muscles can be caused by multiple factors. Stress, anxiety, depression, ergonomics, trauma and overuse are all factors that can attribute to tension headaches. While they can be a nuisance, headaches usually aren't serious and can be treated easily through various techniques.

The chiropractors at Noble Choice Chiropractic have treated headaches with great success through the use of: Active Release Technique®, Graston Technique®, therapeutic taping, ultrasound therapy and chiropractic adjustments.

Migraine Headache

A Migraine headache is a condition caused by abnormal brain activity. The exact event is unclear, but migraines are believed to involve the nervous system and chemicals which affect blood flow in the brain and nearby tissues. Improper alignment of the spine, tight muscles, gender, age, alcohol, stress, and anxiety are all factors which can cause migraines. There are two forms of Migraines, those with auras and those without auras.

The chiropractors at Noble Choice Chiropractic have treated migraine headaches with great success through the use of: Active Release Technique®, Graston Technique®, therapeutic taping, and chiropractic adjustments.

Plantar Fasciitis

Plantar Fasciitis is a condition that has many causes. Some include: Overuse of the foot or calf, improper ergonomics, and muscle tightness, just to name a few. Many people are treated for Plantar Fasciitis only on the foot but fail to receive treatment on the calf as well. The plantar fascia is located on the bottom of the foot and runs from the heel to the toes. When a patient's calf tightens up, it pulls on the heel through the Achilles tendon, this pull puts a stretch on the plantar fascia resulting in the pain throughout the heel and bottom of the foot.

The chiropractors at Noble Choice Chiropractic have treated Plantar Fasciitis with success through the use of: Active Release Technique®, Graston Technique®, therapeutic taping, ultrasound therapy and chiropractic adjustments.
